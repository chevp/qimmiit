# Browser Naming - Inuit/Inuktitut-Inspired

## Concept Overview

For a rendering-browser concept, we want naming that captures:

- **Seeing** or **viewing** (since a browser is about looking/navigating visuals)
- **Container** or **space** where rendering happens
- **Window**, **lens**, or **eye** metaphors

## Inuit/Inuktitut Words & Concepts

| Word | Syllabics | Meaning | Notes |
|------|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Qilak** | ᕿᓚᒃ | Sky, horizon | Symbolizing a wide view, open space to see |
| **Iglu** | ᐃᒡᓗ | House or shelter | Metaphor for a container or space |
| **Qaujimajatuqangit** | ᖃᐅᔨᒪᔭᑐᖃᙱᑦ | Traditional knowledge or wisdom | Maybe too long but great for something knowledge-related |
| **Ulluq** | ᐅᓪᓗᖅ | Eye | Natural viewer of the world |
| **Siqiniq** | ᓯᖅᓂᖅ | Light | Emphasizes rendering/visualization |
| **Nunaq** | ᓄᓇᖅ | Land or ground | The base or foundation |

## Rendering-Browser Name Ideas

| Name Idea | Meaning / Reasoning |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Qilak** | The horizon or wide sky — a window to see and explore visuals. A poetic "rendering viewport." |
| **Ulluq** | Eye — the natural viewer or browser of the world. Could be a neat metaphor for rendering display. |
| **Iglu-view** | House or container for rendering content, like a "rendering house" or "viewport." |
| **Siqiniq-Viewer** | Light-viewer, emphasizing rendering/light visualization. |
| **Qangatasuuq** | Window in Inuit — literal window metaphor for a browser. |

## Hybrid Names (Inuit roots + English suffixes)

- **QilakView**
- **UlluqBrowse**
- **IgluRender**
- **SiqiniqScan**

## Style Considerations

Names can be crafted to match different vibes:
- **Poetic** - Emphasizes cultural metaphor and meaning
- **Techy** - Modern software conventions
- **Minimalist** - Short and simple
